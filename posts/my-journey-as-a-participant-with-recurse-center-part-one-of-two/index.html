My journey as a participant with Recurse Center Part One of Two

An overview of my first six weeks at the Recurse Center, the projects I've worked on and the things I've learned.
March 28, 2021
Table of Contents
  • Learning more about Generative Art
  • Changing my website to Hugo Blog
  • Athena flights Next.js project

Time really flies! It’s been six weeks since I’ve become a participant at the Recurse Center (RC). It’s an amazing space for new or experienced programmers, developers, or anyone who wants to spend time learning more about code. I’ve met so many motivated and talented individuals whom have inspired me to build more and code more frequently.

I signed up to participate from February 15th to May 7th or the “Spring 1 2021 batch” as we call it. The first couple week of RC was a bit overwhelming especially since everything is virtual at the moment and all of my interactions are via zoom and chat on RC’s zulip platform. I didn’t work on my personal projects for the first week but I did pair program on a game of life problem and did a few leetcode problems collaboratively with others RC members. This definitely opened up my perspective on how others program and also gave me reassurance that I’m learning the right things and adhering to good coding conventions. Most coding best practices are subjective and really depends on your use case.

Learning more about Generative Art#

I learned more about generative art and now I’m really interested in trying to create my own works some day. I’m definitely going to learn more about p5.js. I was introduced to the works of Tyler Hobbs and really like how he tries to imitate the qualities of traditional mediums like watercolor. The colors he chooses are energetic but at the same time, calming. I would love to hang one up in my house in the future.

Changing my website to Hugo Blog#

I also got inspired to transform this website (linnali.com) from a html,css,javascript portfolio website to a hugo blog. I considered hugo prior to RC but I decided not to use it because it looked too plain but after seeing a fellow RC’er hugo blog I decided to convert. I liked the minimalism of it but you can add some customizations after you’re familiar with the theme structure. The important thing is that it’s a good place to start if you want to write blog posts and publish fast. I got caught up in designing my site a little more so it took longer. I watched Mike Dane’s Hugo Tutorial Series to get a basic understanding of how themes work and how to deploy. I will likely migrate this site to be a Next.js blog but for now hugo is a great template.

Athena flights Next.js project#

For the past two weeks I’ve been working on Athena flights (you can find it on my Software Projects page.) It’s been a great learning experience. I was new to Next.js, Typescript, GraphQL, and Postgres so this project was a great way for me to start working with these technologies with another RC’er who has built another project with this tech stack. From this project I realized that having someone walk though a piece of code you’re working is immensely more helpful and speeds up your learning way more than just watching tutorials on the topic.

RC is just a great place to learn from others even if they are on topics you’re not ever going to dig into. Just be careful to stay on track and prioritize your own personal goals before you get enticed down rabbit holes of other people’s passion projects. You can find people working in a variety of different programming languages (C, Haskell, Python, Javascript/Typescript, Java, Lisp, Rust, Scala, …etc). There’s presentations every friday for those who want to share their work and I’ve seen amazing project from web apps and games to bots and a small scale database.

If this sounds like a group you’d like to be a part of you can apply at RC's application page . It’s a great time. It’s completely free and since it’s all virtual you will not have to pay for NYC rent. Usually the program is held in-person and you’d have to either live or relocate to NYC but at the moment it is open to everyone around the world!

I still have six more weeks in of my batch left and I will use this time to work on more coding project with my fellow RC members and learn more programming through zoom coffee chats and pair programming. But after my batch ends I’ll still keep learning and collaborate with others because our motto is to “Never Graduate”!
